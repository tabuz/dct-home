Most finance directors in construction distribution know the problem intimately: a spreadsheet that lives on one person's laptop, a month-end process that takes the better part of two weeks, and a nagging suspicion that the numbers aren't quite right.

Rebate reconciliation is, quietly, one of the largest sources of financial leakage in the industry.

## The Anatomy of the Problem

Supplier rebate agreements in construction distribution are rarely simple. A single supplier relationship might carry a dozen different rebate tiers — volume thresholds, growth bonuses, promotional funds, early-payment discounts — each with its own calculation logic, reporting period, and claim deadline.

When this logic lives in spreadsheets rather than software, three things happen:

**First, it becomes person-dependent.** The person who built the spreadsheet is the only one who fully understands it. Their absence — through illness, holiday, or resignation — creates a crisis.

**Second, it becomes error-prone.** Manual data entry across multiple ERP exports, combined with formula complexity that accretes over years, means errors are not occasional. They are endemic. Industry estimates suggest 3–5% of rebate value is routinely under-claimed or misclaims that result in supplier disputes.

**Third, it becomes invisible.** Because the process is opaque, senior leadership has no real-time view of rebate accruals. Cash flow forecasts are imprecise. Negotiating power in supplier reviews is weakened because you can't demonstrate the true commercial relationship in numbers.

## What an Automated Commercial Engine Looks Like

When we built the Commercial Engine for Pam Building, the mandate was clear: eliminate the spreadsheet entirely without touching their SAP environment.

The architecture we designed operates in three layers:

**Data extraction.** A middleware layer runs against their SAP system on a defined schedule, pulling transaction data across relevant purchase categories and applying the rebate tier logic we'd encoded from their supplier agreements. No manual export. No copy-paste.

**Automated calculation and accrual.** Rebate values are calculated continuously, not at month-end. Finance has a live dashboard showing accrued but unclaimed rebate by supplier, by period, and by agreement type. The month-end process went from twelve days to a review and approval workflow measured in hours.

**Self-service supplier portal.** Distributors can log in to review claimback submissions, upload supporting documentation, and track payment status without a single phone call to the finance team. Dispute resolution time fell significantly because both parties are working from the same data.

## The Investment Case

The ROI calculation for this type of project is unusually straightforward. It has three components:

- **Recovered under-claims.** For a business turning over £50M with 4% average rebate yield, recovering even 2% of what was being left on the table represents £40,000 per year. In most cases the recovery is considerably higher.
- **Finance team time.** A two-week manual process, across two or three people, represents roughly forty person-days per year. At senior finance rates, that's a tangible cost that disappears entirely.
- **Cash flow accuracy.** When you can see rebate accruals in real time, your treasury function improves. That has a value that's harder to quantify but entirely real.
